What is a Rubber Extruder Machine?

A rubber extruder machine is a continuous processing system that converts raw rubber compounds into specific profiles, shapes, and forms through a screw and barrel mechanism. The rotating screw pushes the rubber through a die, creating consistent cross-sectional products like hoses, seals, tubes, and tire components.

Cold feed extruders accept pre-mixed rubber directly from a mixing mill without preheating, offering energy efficiency and consistent output. Our extruders feature precise temperature control, variable speed drives, and interchangeable dies for various applications.

We also manufacture rubber mixing mills and dispersion kneaders for complete rubber processing solutions. Contact our engineering team for custom configurations.

Key Extruder Features:

  • Cold feed design - no preheating required
  • Multi-zone temperature control (50°C to 250°C)
  • Variable Frequency Drive for precise speed control
  • Interchangeable dies for multiple profiles
  • Energy efficient operation

Applications of Rubber Extruder Machines

Automotive

Seals, gaskets, weather strips, hoses, and tire treads

Industrial

Profiles, tubes, cables sheathing, and conveyor belts

Construction

Window seals, door gaskets, and expansion joints
