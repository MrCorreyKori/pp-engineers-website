What is a Rubber Mixing Mill?

A rubber mixing mill is a heavy-duty industrial machine used for processing and compounding rubber materials. It consists of two counter-rotating rolls that create shear force to mix, blend, and plasticize rubber compounds with various additives, fillers, and chemicals.

The adjustable roll gap allows precise control over sheet thickness, while the friction ratio between rolls ensures efficient shearing and dispersion. Modern mixing mills feature advanced safety mechanisms, variable speed control, and integrated cooling systems for optimal performance. Our 22x60 model runs on bearings and saves up to 15% power compared to conventional bull gear mills.

We also manufacture rubber dispersion kneaders and extrusion systems for complete rubber processing solutions. Contact our engineering team for custom configurations.

Key Working Principles:

  • Counter-rotating rolls create shear force for mixing
  • Friction ratio (typically 1:1.2 to 1:1.25) ensures efficient compounding
  • Adjustable nip gap controls sheet thickness
  • Integrated cooling prevents overheating during processing

Applications of Rubber Mixing Mill

Tire Manufacturing

Tread compounds, sidewall rubber, inner liners, and bead fillers

Industrial Rubber Products

Conveyor belts, hoses, gaskets, seals, and rubber sheets
